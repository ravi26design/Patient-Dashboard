Quick Start Guide
Five steps to get going
Welcome to Rudra 👋
Your recovery companion — here every day, with a real human always one tap away. You don't have to do it all at once. Here's how to get started.
Get going in 5 steps
1 Do your first check-inTap Reflect on your home screen. It takes about a minute — your medication, how you're feeling, sleep, and cravings, ending with a short note in your own words. Every check-in earns XP and helps your care team show up for you.
2 Know where Help livesThe bright Help button sits at the top of every screen. Tap it any time to reach the 988 Lifeline, call 911, open your urge toolkit, or reach your own people.
3 When an urge hits, open your toolkitTap the Help button for your urge toolkit — a calming breathing guide, your own relief activities, and one-tap ways to reach your people. Most cravings peak and pass in 15–30 minutes — Rudra helps you ride it out.
4 Stay on your medicationYour MOUD (like Suboxone) shows on your home screen and is the very first question in each check-in. Mark every dose to keep your streak going and keep your care team in the loop — no judgment either way.
5 Earn rewards and bring in your peopleEvery action earns XP toward levels — Seed → Bloom → Flourish. Family and sponsors can pledge real rewards on your Rewards board and cheer you on. Recovery is easier together.
🌿 One small thing counts. A single check-in today is a win. Come back tomorrow and you've started a streak.
User Manual
Every feature, explained
Your complete guide to Rudra
Everything your recovery companion can do, organized by where you'll find it. Skim it now, or come back whenever you need it.
1 · Getting set up
Your account
You'll verify your phone number, add a few details, and agree to plain-language privacy terms. Records about substance use get extra federal protection under 42 CFR Part 2 & HIPAA — nothing is shared without your consent.
Triggers & relief
Name what tends to spark a craving (a feeling, place, person, or time of day) and what helps you cope. Rudra uses these to warn you early and to stock your urge toolkit. You can edit both anytime in your profile.
Connecting your care team
The final setup step. Enter your clinic's name or the code they gave you to securely sync your appointments and MOUD plan. You're always in control of what's shared — and you can connect later if you don't have it handy.
2 · Home — your recovery at a glance
Recovery Health score
A 0–100 gauge that blends your check-ins, medication, and activity into one friendly number. "Above Average" is a good day — but the trend matters far more than any single score.
Recovery Today
Four daily actions, each worth XP:
Insights — a personalized read on your day
Reflect — your daily check-in
Connect — share and connect with peers in Community (usernames only)
Activities — a guided exercise to steady you
Journey, streaks & medication
See your days in recovery and progress to your next milestone, your streaks by focus area, and your next MOUD dose — all on one screen.
3 · The daily check-in (Reflect)
About seven quick steps, roughly a minute, and it earns XP. The order is:
Medication — did you take your MOUD today?
Substance use — anything in the last 24 hours
Cravings — how strong, and how you resisted
Mood & feelings — how the day felt
Body — sleep and pain
The week ahead — motivation and confidence
Reflection — a note in your own words, typed or spoken
Every answer is confidential and shared only with your care team. There's no judgment on anything you report.
4 · Getting help fast
The Help button
Always at the top of every screen. It opens your crisis hub:
I'm in crisis — 988 Lifeline, crisis text line & naloxone help
Call 911 — for an overdose or medical emergency
I'm having an urge — opens your urge toolkit
Your support team — real people, one tap away
Your urge toolkit
Breathing, grounding, urge surfing, a distraction game, and your own saved relief activities — plus one-tap calling or texting the people you trust.
Naloxone (Narcan)
Keep your naloxone status current and learn the 5 steps to respond to an overdose. It's shared with your care team so they can help you stay prepared.
5 · Activities, Progress & Community
Activities
Activities picked for you each day, matched to your Recovery Health score — from urge surfing on harder days to gratitude and planning on stronger ones. Complete them to earn points.
Progress
Your trends over time — urges, triggers, mood, and sleep — so you and your care team can see what's working and plan for the harder moments.
Community
A peer discussion board where members share wins, questions, and encouragement. Only usernames are shown — real names are never displayed.
6 · Rewards & your people
Rewards
Earn XP for every recovery action and level up — Seed → Bloom → Flourish. Family and sponsors can pledge real-world rewards on your board and send gratitude gifts to keep you going.
Support team & peers
Add the people you trust to call or text in one tap. You control who can talk with your clinical team, per person.
7 · Profile, settings & privacy
Your profile holds your medication, relief activities, support team, care team, and connected family — all editable.
Settings — reminders, accessibility & display, language, and your info
Privacy & Data Sharing — control exactly what's shared, download your data, or delete your account
Nothing about your recovery reaches your care team unless you turn it on.
8 · Screenings you may see
From time to time Rudra may invite you to short check-ins — wellness & social needs, mood, or withdrawal — that help your team tailor your care and connect you with resources like housing, food, or transportation.
Rudra is a recovery companion and does not replace professional medical care. If you're in danger or think you may be overdosing, call 911. For emotional crisis, call or text 988. Learning content is clinician-reviewed.

Alert
Gentle nudges, right when they help
🔔 What "Alert" does
Alert is a kind, quiet nudge that appears right after your daily check-in — only on the days you might need a little extra support.
When it shows up
Right after you finish a check-in, Rudra blends your answers into your Recovery Score. If that score dips below "good" for the day, a gentle Alert appears. On strong days, it stays quiet — it will never nag you.
What it offers
Two or three small, doable activities — matched to how you actually feel today:
High cravings → your urge toolkit (breathing, grounding, a quick game)
Low or heavy mood → a voice journal or a peer who gets it
Stress or anxiety → a short breathing exercise
Low motivation → a 2-minute lesson or a look at your streaks
Plus your own relief activities — the things you told us help you
Points for showing up
Each suggestion shows the XP you'll earn for doing it. Finishing an activity gently nudges your score back up — and your care team sees the effort you're putting in.
Always your choice
A lower day is information, not failure. Alert is never a judgment. You can act on a suggestion or simply tap "Maybe later" — you're always in control.
Want to see it? Finish a daily check-in from your home screen. If your score lands below "good," your Alert will appear.

Recovery Score
Your day, in one friendly number
📊 Your Recovery Score
One number, 0–100, that turns your daily check-in into a simple snapshot of how your recovery is going today.
What goes into it
Your check-in answers, gently weighted toward what matters most in recovery: taking your medication, cravings, any substance use, mood, sleep, stress, and your confidence for the week ahead.
How to read the gauge
The dial runs from 0 on the left to 100 on the right, across five bands. Higher means steadier. A quick guide:
80–100 · High
60–79 · Above Average
40–59 · Average
20–39 · Below Average
0–19 · Low
What matters most
The trend over days — not any single number. One low day is completely normal and doesn't erase your progress. Watch the direction, not the dot.
How it moves
Honest check-ins keep it accurate. Staying on your medication and completing activities — especially the ones your Alert suggests — gently nudge it up over time.
Your score is for you first. It's a compass for your own reflection, and — only with your permission — a way for your care team to support you between visits.
The score is a supportive estimate, not a medical diagnosis. Always follow your care team's guidance.
Rewards
Your effort, celebrated
🏆 How Rewards works
Recovery is real work. Rewards makes that work visible and celebrated — every healthy step earns points, and the people who love you can cheer you on.
Earn points every day
Small actions add up to XP. The daily ones:
Daily check-in — your biggest daily reward, and the heart of your recovery
Take your medication
Share or connect in the Community board
Use a relief tool or ride out an urge
Start with your check-in
If you do one thing a day, make it your daily check-in. It earns the most points, keeps your Recovery Score honest, and helps your care team show up for you — all in about a minute.
Level up as you grow
Your points move you through levels — Seed → Bloom → Flourish — a gentle picture of how far you've come. Streaks track your consistency, one day at a time.
Bring in your people
Recovery is easier together. On your Reward Board, the people you invite — family, a sponsor, or a peer — can:
Pledge a real-world reward (a dinner, a gift card) that unlocks when you hit a milestone
Send gratitude gifts and encouragement
Celebrate your streaks and level-ups with you
Private by design
You're always in control of who's involved and what they see. The people you invite see your milestones and streaks — never your private check-in answers or notes. Nothing is shared with anyone, including your care team, unless you turn it on in Privacy settings.
Find it all under Rewards in the bottom bar — your level, your streaks, and your Reward Board.
Rewards are here to encourage you, never to pressure you. Your recovery is yours — go at your own pace. 🌿
